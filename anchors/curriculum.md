## the rules (what makes this one stick)
1. time-boxed: 60-90 min/day, 5 days/wk. done > perfect.
2. artifact or it didn't happen: every session ends with something in
   00-inbox/study/ - solved problems, a proof attempt, code, wrong ideas.
3. problems:reading = 2:1. reading feels like progress; problems are.
4. one pure track + one applied track + one dessert track. never more.
5. Coach compiles coach/progress.md nightly + spaced-review queue.
   Almanac flags 7 days of silence. Sunrise nags with the due reviews.

## phase 0 - proof bootcamp (now, ~3 weeks)
- Hammack, Book of Proof (free pdf) - fast pass, do the exercises
- Natural Number Game (Lean, browser) - proofs as programming, finish it
- exit test: prove sqrt(2) irrational + induction on your own, clean

## phase 1 (~4 months)
- pure: Abbott, Understanding Analysis + Francis Su's lectures (YouTube)
  - stuck? Cummings, Real Analysis: A Long-Form Textbook
- applied: Ross, A First Course in Probability (feeds ML AND Shreve)
- dessert: Karpathy, Zero to Hero (micrograd -> nanoGPT) - build every one

## phase 2 (~4 months)
- pure: MacKay, Information Theory, Inference, and Learning Algorithms
  (free pdf + his Cambridge lectures) - the perpetual-relevant one
- applied: Shreve, Stochastic Calculus for Finance I
- dessert: Prince, Understanding Deep Learning (free pdf), implement chapters

## phase 3 (~4 months)
- pure: Sipser, Theory of Computation (computability -> Busy Beaver)
- applied: Hull (options) -> Sinclair (volatility trading);
  Damodaran's NYU valuation lectures (YouTube) alongside
- dessert: paper-trade the bimodal thesis; log every trade to ledger/

## phase 4 (the frontier)
- Fong & Spivak, Seven Sketches (free) + their MIT lecture videos
- fixed-point theory (Lawvere) - the theorem this whole system runs on
- Li & Vitanyi (AIT) - entry via MacKay's Kolmogorov chapters
- Parr/Pezzulo/Friston, Active Inference (open access)
- Taleb, Dynamic Hedging; Penrose alongside as bedtime philosophy
